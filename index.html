A City Above

A City Above

‘A City Above’ represents a concept that elegantly shifts the notion of standard living, improving the speed and efficiency of Sydney’s rail network whilst simultaneously encouraging the nature of community and social interaction and also allowing for further housing space to be allocated. The concept was formed through analyzing my data set which demonstrated the distribution of transport use according to suburb, where problems and a solution was then constructed.

Problems

Over Population

Overpopulation is a current issue facing Sydney where the density of people in Sydney continue to increase as more people need to live close to the city resulting in more apartments block, i.e urban consolidation.

Train Delays

Sydney railway faces the consistent regular issue of being slow and delayed due to an outdated system and poor placement of track lines.

Road Congestion

As population increases and a slow unreliable train system is in place, the amount of motor vehicles on the road continues to increase.

Poor Cycling Accessibility

Sydney has a terrible system of cycling accessibility resulting in a very small number of individuals cycling to work.
